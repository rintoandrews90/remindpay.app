# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Apple device owners who have been burned — a late fee, a missed warranty claim, a subscription they forgot to cancel. They're not disorganised by nature; they just have too many things to track and no single place to track them. The emotional job: relief and control. They come to RemindPay after a painful moment, and the marketing site's job is to meet them there and make them feel like someone finally built this for them.

## Product Purpose

Marketing landing page for RemindPay, an iOS bill and warranty tracker. The site's sole conversion goal is App Store downloads. Success means a visitor who felt the pain of missing a payment or claim lands here, immediately recognises their problem reflected back at them, trusts that this app is serious, and taps "Download."

## Brand Personality

Calm, reliable, clear. The voice is the opposite of a startup marketing blast — it's the tone of a tool that quietly does its job well. Confidence without noise. Trustworthy without being clinical or cold.

## Anti-references

- **Overly playful / cartoon**: No mascots, no loud illustration styles, no Duolingo-energy. A bill tracker isn't a game.
- **Busy subscription-app clutter**: No sections competing for attention. No feature-dumping. Every element on the page should earn its space; if cutting it wouldn't be noticed, cut it.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with relief, not features** — the hero and every section opener should speak to the emotional outcome (peace of mind, never missing again) before enumerating capabilities.
2. **One idea per section** — no section tries to do two things. Focus, then transition cleanly.
3. **Apple-ecosystem native** — design decisions should feel at home next to App Store screenshots and iOS UI conventions. Nothing here should feel out of place on an Apple product page.
4. **Calm confidence** — typography, colour, and spacing choices signal authority without shouting. White space is not wasted space.
5. **Clarity always wins** — if a label, headline, or CTA needs to be read twice, rewrite it. No clever wordplay at the cost of immediate understanding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Minimum WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on all text.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — all scroll-reveal and floating animations have a reduced-motion fallback (already implemented via Motion's `inView()` and the `.no-motion` class).
- Colour is never the sole carrier of meaning (accent tints supplement icons and labels, not replace them).
